Books by Chinua Achebe

  • Things Fall Apart (1958) - Achebe's debut novel that explores the impact of colonialism on Igbo society through the life of Okonkwo.
  • No Longer at Ease (1960) - A sequel to Things Fall Apart, focusing on the challenges faced by a young Nigerian man educated in England as he navigates corruption and cultural conflict.
  • Arrow of God (1964) - A novel that delves into the clash between traditional Igbo religion and Christianity during colonial rule.
  • Anthills of the Savannah (1987) - A novel that explores the political and social upheaval in post-colonial Nigeria.
  • A Man of the People (1966) - A satirical novel that critiques the corruption and moral decay in post-independence African politics.
  • The Education of a British-Protected Child (2009) - A collection of essays reflecting on Achebe's life, work, and views on African literature and culture.
  • There Was a Country: A Personal History of Biafra (2012) - A memoir recounting Achebe's experiences during the Nigerian Civil War and the Biafran struggle for independence.
  • Collected Poems (2009) - A compilation of Achebe's poetry, showcasing his lyrical exploration of themes such as identity, culture, and social justice.
  • Hopes and Impediments: Selected Essays (1988) - A collection of essays discussing African literature, culture, and the challenges faced by post-colonial societies.
